2017 Greetings from the Winfield Historical Society and Museum,

It's membership renewal time for us and we look forward to your support.  Our survival depends solely on membership, donations and memorials.  In order for us to preserve our history of Winfield it is imperative that we acquire new members.  Please encourage friends and relatives to help us in continuing our local history of Winfield.  These are some things that were accomplishments in 2016 and some continued goals for 2017.
  • Basement floor painted (Erickson's Toyland)
  • New shelving added in the basement
  • World War I program presented by Judy Rawson.  Judy's Grandfather (Herb Butler) served in France - this program was very well received.
  • Monthly window displays
  • Continued work on identifying buildings on main street and their ownership/occupancy history
  • Continued work on research of Winfield history to be made into a mini-movie or power point
  • New display of rolling pins and kitchen utensils in the back room.
  • New display of World War I memorabilia in the enclosed display in the back room
  • Continuing to collect memorabilia shared with us
  • Answering requests from relatives searching for genealogy help
This year we will be having a program on Vietnam.  It will be on Sunday, February 5, 2017 at 2:00 p.m. at the Winfield City Building (Legion).  Mike Sells will be presenting the program and we hope other Vietnam veterans will join us.  This is open to the public and refreshments will be served.  A free will donation is appreciated. Please mark your calendars and join us.

Visit the museum on Mondays from 10:00 a.m. to 12:00 p.m
