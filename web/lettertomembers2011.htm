January 17, 2011

Dear fellow citizens of Winfield,

As we begin another year, we seek your interest, your participation and your membership. This is a key time in the preservation of Winfield history. It has never been more important, more urgent, than it is right now to gather and preserve the facts and memorabilia of our everyday lives.

If you're not already, we invite you to become familiar with Winfield Historical Society & Museum in the Masonic Temple, 114 South Locust. Stop in any Monday 10 a.m. to noon, or join us any fourth Monday at 7 p.m. We would welcome and appreciate your input, help, or support.

2010 was a good year for us. We provided the community with fascinating programs and look forward to doing the same in 2011.

Our annual Railroad Day, titled Importance of Civil War Railroads, will be held Monday, February 21st at 1 pm in the WMU small gymnasium. Admission is free, and complimentary refreshments will be served. There will be extra tables if you would like to bring some railroad paraphernalia for Show and Tell.

All our projects and programs would be impossible without the support received from members and friends of the Winfield Historical Society.

The past few years have been financially hard on museums, so your gifts and membership is especially needed in order to continue providing a museum and programs related to our local history.

Please hear our plea and become a member. Join Winfield Historical Society's endeavor to gather and preserve Winfield's (YOUR) history.
