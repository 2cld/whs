2015 has flown by and it is membership renewal time already!  We continue, with your support, to have a museum and historical society in Winfield.  Your support is necessary and greatly appreciated.

As we look back over the past year, we are saddened by the loss of founding board member Bill Klopfenstein.  He is greatly missed and was an invaluable historian and researcher.  He wrote many of our booklets and also, with Gary Miller's help, made our prized replica of the 1911 Curtis Pusher Biplane that landed in Winfield.

We were very happy to have Janet Everett and Gary Huston on our board this year.  They are a great addition to our efforts.  Steve Klopfenstein will be joining us this year as a new board member.  We also worked on updating our by-laws and web site.  Judith Robinette continues to oversee the website and facebook page.  She does a great job so please take time to visit our sites.

Erickson's Toy Room got a new wall and we hope to paint the floor soon.  Thanks to Gary Long and Gary Huston for that work.  The store room also is getting cleaned and is an ongoing job to keep organized.

This year we will be removing the Civil War display and replacing it with World War I memorabilia.  If you haven't seen the Civil War items yet, be sure to stop in and see it while you still can.  We will be sponsoring a program on World War I this winter at a date to be determined.

We are always very happy to get new things to display.  Please keep us in mind.  We are proud of the items we received from the Dr. Chance andMarjorie Hull estates.  We also hosted the Model A Car Club, from Cedar Rapids this past September.

2016 looks to be a great year with more of our history to share.  Please take advantage of all we have at the museum and help us to continue our efforts.  There is much to be learned by the life and times of days gone by, let us save our history together.

Visit the museum on Mondays from 10:00 a.m. to 12:00 p.m.  Our web-site is www.winfieldhistoricalsociety.com or visit us on Facebook at facebook.com/winfieldhistoricalsociety.
